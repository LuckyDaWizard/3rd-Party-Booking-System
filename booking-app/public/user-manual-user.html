Role: User (Nurse / Reception)

CareFirst Third Party Booking System

Day-to-day guide for staff who create patient bookings.
Version 1 · Last updated 2026-06-08

You are a User. Your job in this system is to create patient bookings, capture payment, and hand the patient over to the consulting nurse via “Start Consult”. Everything you can and can’t do is described in this manual. If a menu item or button isn’t mentioned here, it’s not part of your role.

1. Signing in

Open the booking system in your browser. Your manager will give you the address. The sign-in page asks for two things:

  1. Email
    The work email address your manager registered for you. It is not case-sensitive.
  2. PIN
    A 4-digit number. You set this when you first signed in; if you have forgotten it, see “Forgotten PIN” below.
  3. Click “Sign in”
    If both are correct, you land on the Home dashboard. If either is wrong, you see “Invalid email or PIN.” After 5 wrong PIN attempts in a row, the system locks your account for 15 minutes — this protects you, but you must wait.
Important Never share your PIN. Every booking, payment, and Start Consult is logged against the user who was signed in. If someone else uses your PIN, their actions will appear as yours.

2. The Home dashboard

Home is what you see immediately after signing in. It shows:

  • A list of recent bookings created at your unit.
  • A “Create a Booking” button to start a new one.
  • The sidebar on the left with everything you can navigate to.

Click any booking in the list to see its details. The status of each booking is shown as a coloured pill:

StatusWhat it means
In ProgressYou started the booking but haven’t completed payment yet. You can resume.
Payment CompletePayment received. The patient can be started on consultation.
SuccessfulConsultation started (Start Consult was clicked and CareFirst accepted the hand-off).
DiscardedYou explicitly cancelled the booking before payment.
AbandonedBooking was left unfinished and the browser was closed. The system marks these automatically after a short delay.

3. Switching units

If you work at more than one clinic unit, the sidebar shows your active unit near the top. Click on it to open the “Switch unit” page and choose a different one. Every booking you create from that point forward is attached to the unit you have selected.

Why this matters Bookings and payments are all scoped to the unit you were on at the time. Switch before you start a booking, not halfway through.

4. Creating a booking — overview

A booking is the record of one patient’s visit. Creating one walks you through five steps in order:

  1. Patient search — find the patient (or confirm they’re new).
  2. Patient details — basic info, address, contact, ID verification.
  3. Patient metrics — vital signs (blood pressure, weight, etc). May be skipped at some clinics.
  4. Payment — PayFast, self-collect, or monthly invoice depending on the clinic.
  5. Confirmation & Start Consult — the patient gets handed over to CareFirst.

You can leave a booking part-way through and come back to it from the Home page; the system saves your progress every couple of seconds.

5. Step 1 · Patient search

Click Create a Booking from the sidebar or the Home page. The first screen asks you to identify the patient. You have three search options:

  1. By National ID
    South African 13-digit ID number. The system checks the digit pattern as you type.
  2. By Passport
    For foreign nationals. Free-text; type the passport number exactly as it appears.
  3. By Date of Birth
    Use this when the patient doesn’t have or remember their ID number. If more than one patient matches, the system will show a list and ask you to pick the right one.

If your clinic uses Nurse Verification, an extra prompt asks for a verification code before you can continue. Your own PIN works here — as long as you’re assigned to this unit you can sign off your own booking; you don’t need to ask a manager. (A manager or admin assigned to the unit can also enter theirs.) Some clinics turn this prompt off entirely.

What the search actually looks at The search only checks patients whose previous bookings were made at one of the units you’re assigned to. It does not look across the whole system.
  • If the patient was previously booked at this unit or another of your units → they’ll be found and pre-filled.
  • If they were only ever booked at a different clinic / a unit you’re not assigned to → the search returns nothing, and that’s expected — enter their details fresh. They’re still recognised downstream by their ID number, so nothing is duplicated at the consultation stage.
  • The search is case-insensitive for names and passports, but it does look for an exact spelling of the name (partial matches aren’t supported yet).
Tip If the patient is found, their details are pre-filled in the next step — you only need to confirm. If not found, you fill them in from scratch.

6. Step 2 · Patient details

This is the longest step. It is broken into four sub-tabs:

  1. Basic Info
    First names, surname, title, ID type, ID number / passport, date of birth, gender, nationality. Fields highlight red if invalid (e.g. wrong ID digit-count).
  2. Address
    Street, city, province, country, postal code. Province auto-suggests from postal code when you tab out.
  3. Contact
    Mobile, alternative phone, email. Mobile must start with +27 or a leading 0 for South African numbers.
  4. Verification
    Confirm consent and (where enabled) upload an ID photo or capture a signature. The Save & Continue button only enables once every required field is valid.
Auto-save Every change is saved to the booking in the background. If you accidentally close the tab, come back via Home → the in-progress booking and you’ll find your work intact.

7. Step 3 · Patient metrics

Capture vital signs: blood pressure (systolic / diastolic), pulse, temperature, weight, height. Each field has a sensible range; the form warns you (but does not block you) if a value is out of range — in case the reading really is unusual.

Note Some clinics have this step turned off at the client level. If so, you won’t see it — the system will jump straight to the Payment step.

8. Step 4 · Payment

The system picks one of three payment modes automatically based on how the clinic is set up. You don’t get to choose — the right one is shown.

Mode A · PayFast (most common)

Click Pay Now. You are redirected to the PayFast hosted payment page where the patient enters their card or selects another payment method. After payment succeeds, PayFast brings you back to a confirmation screen. The patient receives a PayFast email receipt directly from PayFast.

Important If you close the tab during the PayFast step and reopen the booking, the system will automatically check with PayFast whether payment went through. You don’t need to do anything special.

Mode B · Self-collect

Some clinics collect payment themselves at the counter (cash, card-on-site, etc.) and just record it here. You confirm the amount and click Mark as Paid.

Mode C · Monthly invoice

Some clinics are billed monthly by First Care Solutions rather than per booking. You see a notice saying so and click Continue — no money is collected at this step.

Coupon codes (optional, gateway only)

If the clinic is configured to accept coupons, you’ll see an Apply coupon box on the PayFast payment screen. Type the code, press Apply, and the discount is taken off the total immediately. If the code is invalid, expired, or has run out of uses, you’ll see an inline error and nothing on the booking changes.

If the coupon takes the total to R0 The Pay With PayFast button is automatically replaced by a green Complete Free Booking button. Click it and the booking completes without ever leaving the page — no PayFast redirect, no card details. The booking shows up on Patient History with an emerald “Comped” chip.
If the patient leaves before paying Don’t worry about the coupon. When the booking later flips to Abandoned (idle timeout) or Discarded (Options → Discard Flow), the coupon usage is automatically released — the same code can be applied again on the patient’s next attempt, limits permitting.

9. Step 5 · Confirmation & Start Consult

Once payment is confirmed (or skipped for self-collect / monthly-invoice clients), you reach the success screen. From here:

  1. Review the booking summary
    Confirm everything is correct before handing off.
  2. Accept terms
    The patient (or you on their behalf) ticks the consent boxes.
  3. Click “Start Consult”
    The system sends the patient’s details across to CareFirst Patient (the consultation app). If CareFirst is reachable, the booking flips to Successful and you’re done. The patient is now in CareFirst’s queue.
If Start Consult fails You’ll see a red banner explaining the problem. The booking stays at Payment Complete and you can retry. If it keeps failing, raise an incident with your manager — payment has been taken; nothing is lost.

10. Patient History

The Patient History page (sidebar → Patient History) shows every booking ever created at your unit, with filters for date, patient name, ID, and status.

Use it to:

  • Find a returning patient before creating a new booking.
  • Look up an old booking reference for a refund or follow-up.
  • Check the status of a booking you started earlier.

Click any row to open the booking and see the full audit trail (who created it, when, payment status, etc.).

11. Your profile picture

Click your avatar in the top-right corner to open the profile menu. Upload a small photo (JPEG or PNG, under 2 MB). It appears next to your name on every booking you create.

12. Forgotten PIN

  1. Click “Forgot PIN?” on the sign-in page
    Enter your email address.
  2. Check your inbox
    You’ll receive a 6-digit reset code. It is valid for 15 minutes and can be used only once.
  3. Enter the code, then choose a new PIN
    Pick any 4 digits except obvious sequences like 0000 or 1234.
Heads up Resetting your PIN signs you out of every device. You’ll need to sign in fresh with the new PIN.

13. Idle timeout & auto sign-out

If you don’t touch the keyboard or mouse for 5 minutes, a warning appears asking if you’re still there. Click anywhere to dismiss it. If you do nothing for a further 2 minutes (7 minutes total), the system signs you out automatically.

Any patient details you were typing are saved as a draft on the booking and will be there when you sign back in.

14. Common problems

ProblemWhat to do
“Invalid email or PIN” Check the email is spelled correctly and the PIN is right. After 5 failed attempts you are locked out for 15 minutes.
Stuck on the PayFast screen Close the tab, go to Home, click the booking, click Resume. The system will check with PayFast and update the status.
Start Consult fails Click Retry. If still failing, the consultation system (CareFirst) is likely down — let your manager know.
Browser shows “You’ve been signed out” You were idle too long. Sign in again; your in-progress work is preserved.
A booking is showing as Abandoned but I didn’t finish it That’s normal. The system marks bookings as Abandoned when the browser closes mid-flow. You can still resume it — the data is intact.
I can’t find a button described in this manual Check the top of the page — you may be on the wrong unit (see “Switching units”). If still missing, the feature may be turned off for your clinic.
Apply Coupon doesn’t do anything Check the code is typed correctly (case-insensitive). If the inline error says “not valid yet” / “expired” / “limit reached”, the code can’t be applied to this booking — ask the patient if they have another code.

15. What this role cannot do

For transparency, here is what a User role cannot see or do. If you need any of these things, ask your unit manager:

  • Create, edit, or remove other users.
  • Create, edit, or remove units.
  • See or change client settings (branding, payment mode, etc).
  • View the system audit log.
  • View the security dashboard (failed sign-ins, active sessions, etc).
  • Trigger system-wide cleanups or reconciliation runs.
