User Manual
Role: Unit Manager

CareFirst Third Party Booking System

Operational guide for the manager(s) of a clinic unit.
Version 1 · Last updated 2026-06-08

You are a Unit Manager. In addition to doing everything a regular User can do (creating bookings, taking payments, starting consultations), you also manage the users and units that belong to your clinic. This manual focuses on the manager-only sections; for the daily booking flow, refer to the User manual.

1. What you can manage

A Unit Manager has authority over the units they are assigned to — not the whole system. Your assignments are visible at the bottom of the User Management page when an admin grants them. In practice this means:

AreaWhat you can do
Users Create, edit, deactivate, and reset PINs for User accounts that work at your units. You cannot create or edit managers or admins.
Units Edit details of units you are assigned to. You can also add new units — they will automatically be linked to the same client as your other units.
Bookings Create them and view all bookings made at your units (via Home and Patient History).
Client-level settings No access. Branding, payment-mode, self-collect toggle — these belong to the System Admin.

2. Your sidebar

Your sidebar shows the same items as a regular User plus two extras:

  • Home
  • Create a Booking
  • Patient History
  • Unit Management — manager-only
  • User Management — manager-only
Note Client Management, Audit Log and Security do not appear in your sidebar. They are system_admin-only.

3. User Management

Click User Management in the sidebar. You see a list of every user who has ever been assigned to one of your units — including their:

  • Avatar, full name and email.
  • Role (you only see User rows; managers and admins are hidden from you).
  • Status: Active, Inactive, or Pending PIN (new account, hasn’t signed in yet).
  • Last sign-in time and the unit(s) they belong to.

From this page you can:

  • Click any row to open the user’s profile and edit details.
  • Click + Add User to create a new account.
  • Filter by name, email or status.

4. Adding a new user

  1. Click “+ Add User”
    Top right of the User Management page.
  2. Fill in the basic details
    First name, surname, work email. The email is also their sign-in identifier — it must be unique across the whole system.
  3. Pick a role
    Your only option is User. Managers cannot create other managers (only system admins can).
  4. Assign one or more units
    You see a list of your assigned units; tick the ones this user should work at. If the user only works at one unit, tick just that one.
  5. Click “Save”
    The system sends the new user an email containing a one-time link to set their PIN. Until they click it, their status is Pending PIN.
If they don’t get the email Check the spelling of the address, then come back to the user’s profile and click Resend invite. Codes expire after 15 minutes.

5. Resetting a user’s PIN

If a user is locked out or has genuinely forgotten their PIN and can’t use the “Forgot PIN” flow themselves, you can trigger a reset:

  1. Open the user’s profile
    User Management → click the user’s row.
  2. Click “Send PIN reset”
    The user receives a 6-digit code by email, valid for 15 minutes, single-use.
  3. They enter the code on the sign-in page
    They click “Forgot PIN?”, type the code, and set a new 4-digit PIN.
Important Resetting a PIN signs the user out of every device they were on. Make sure they are ready to sign back in before you trigger this.

6. Deactivating a user

When a staff member leaves or moves on, deactivate their account rather than deleting it. Deactivation preserves the audit trail of every booking they ever created, but prevents them from signing in again.

  1. Open their profile
    User Management → click the row.
  2. Toggle “Active” off
    A confirmation box appears. Confirm.
  3. All their open sessions are revoked
    If they were signed in at the time, they are kicked out on their next page load.
Re-activating You can toggle them back to Active later; their old PIN still works.

7. Unit Management

Click Unit Management in the sidebar. You see a list of every unit you are assigned to, with its:

  • Name and short code.
  • Client it belongs to (read-only).
  • Logo and accent colour (read-only — set by the System Admin at client level).
  • Number of active users.
  • Number of bookings this month.

Click any unit row to open and edit it, or click + Add Unit to create a new one.

8. Adding a new unit

  1. Click “+ Add Unit”
    Top right of Unit Management.
  2. Pick the parent client
    You see only the client(s) your other units already belong to. (System Admin defines clients; you can only attach units to ones already in the system.)
  3. Enter unit details
    Unit name, short code (used in booking references), address, contact details.
  4. Click “Create”
    The new unit appears in your list. You can now assign users to it.

9. Editing a unit

Click any unit row to open it. You can edit name, code, address, and contact details. The following cannot be changed by a manager:

  • The parent client (System Admin only).
  • Branding — logo, favicon, accent colour (System Admin only, at client level).
  • Payment mode — PayFast / self-collect / monthly-invoice (System Admin only, at client level).
Why these are admin-only These settings cascade across all units of a client. A manager who can edit their own unit could otherwise accidentally change branding for a sister-unit across the country.

10. Working across multiple units

If you manage more than one unit, the sidebar shows your active unit near the top. Click it to switch. Important: the unit you are on at the time you create a booking is the unit it’s attached to — not the unit selected later.

Patient History and Home both show data only for the currently-active unit. To review another unit, switch to it first.

Patient search on Create Booking works the same way — it only finds patients whose previous bookings are at units you’re assigned to (across all of them, combined). Patients booked only at units you’re not assigned to won’t appear in the search results, even if they exist elsewhere in the system. That’s expected — just enter their details fresh; they’re still matched downstream by their ID number.

11. Monitoring bookings

Use Home and Patient History to keep an eye on activity at your unit. Things to watch for:

  • Bookings stuck on “In Progress” for hours: usually the user left them open. They will auto-flip to Abandoned eventually.
  • A run of Discarded bookings from one user: may indicate confusion. Worth a quick chat.
  • A booking stuck on Payment Complete (not yet Successful): the patient paid but Start Consult never fired. Check with the user; if the consultation system (CareFirst) is down, escalate to a System Admin.
  • Coupon bookings: a small emerald chip under the patient name shows the coupon code — CODE · −R25 means the patient got a discount, CODE · Comped means the coupon covered the full fee and PayFast was skipped entirely. The status pill itself goes emerald for the comped case.
Coupons release automatically on discard If you Discard a booking that has a coupon attached (or it goes Abandoned), the coupon’s usage slot is automatically returned to the pool. You don’t need to manually credit anything — the same code can be applied again on the patient’s next attempt (limits permitting).

12. When your PIN is required

The booking flow itself does not usually need a manager — a regular User assigned to a unit can complete their own bookings end-to-end (nurse verification at the start and Start Consult at the end both accept their own PIN). You only need to step in and enter your Manager PIN for actions that have a higher trust bar:

  • Mark Payment as Confirmed — the supervisor override on a booking when PayFast didn’t notify us automatically.
  • Edit / delete a user, or reset another user’s PIN (User Management).
  • Delete a unit (Unit Management).

For these, the system shows a PIN prompt and your Manager PIN authorises the action. Your PIN is the same one you use to sign in.

Important Don’t give your PIN to a User to type in — come and enter it yourself. Every Manager PIN entry is logged with your name as the authoriser.

13. Common manager problems

ProblemWhat to do
User says they didn’t get an invite email Open their profile, click Resend invite. Check spam folder. Codes expire in 15 minutes.
User can’t sign in (lockout) 5 wrong PINs locks an account for 15 minutes. Either wait, or send them a PIN reset.
I can’t see a unit I should be managing Your assignments are managed by the System Admin. Ask them to add the unit to your assignments.
I need to change the clinic logo / accent colour These are client-level settings, controlled by the System Admin. Send the request to them.
A booking is stuck and the user is in tears Open the booking, check the status. If Payment Complete but no Start Consult: try Start Consult again. If it still fails, CareFirst is likely down — raise an incident with the System Admin.

14. What this role cannot do

For transparency, here’s what a Unit Manager cannot do. For these, you need a System Admin:

  • Create, edit or remove other managers or admins.
  • Create or remove clients.
  • Edit client branding (logo, favicon, accent colour).
  • Change a client’s payment mode (PayFast vs. self-collect vs. monthly invoice).
  • Toggle the “Skip patient metrics” or “Nurse verification” client switches.
  • View the system-wide Audit Log.
  • View the Security dashboard (sessions, failed sign-ins, suspicious activity, etc).
  • Run cleanup sweeps or trigger PayFast reconciliation manually.
  • Process a POPIA right-to-erasure request.
