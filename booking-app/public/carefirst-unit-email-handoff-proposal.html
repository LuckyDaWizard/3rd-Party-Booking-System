Integration Proposal & Questions

Notify the Originating Unit on Script Issue
(CareFirst Patient SSO — Auto-Register Handoff)

Prepared 2026-06-01 by First Care Solutions — 3rd Party Booking System
For the CareFirst Patient technical team

We’d like to extend the SSO auto-register handoff so that the unit that originated the booking also receives a copy of any prescription / script generated during the resulting consultation. This document explains the operational reason, what we already send today, and a short list of concrete questions we need answered on your side before we ship the change on ours.

Background — how the handoff works today

Our 3rd Party Booking System captures a patient’s details, takes payment (via PayFast or per-unit collection), then hands the patient over to CareFirst Patient through the auto-register endpoint. We POST the following shape (abbreviated): 

POST https://<careFirstDomain>/api/external/client-sso/auto-register
x-api-key: <our key>

{
  "clientCode": "<our client code>",
  "planCode": "<our plan code>",
  "uniqueReference": "<booking id>",
  "user": {
    "email":       "<patient email>",    // today: only the patient's
    "cellNumber":  "<patient cell>",
    "idNumber":    "<patient ID>",
    "userProfile": { /* title, firstName, surname, DOB, gender, address... */ }
  },
  "returnUrl": "<our booking-system URL>"
}

On success you return a redirect URL we open in a new tab to launch the consultation. The patient receives the script directly from CareFirst Patient (email / SMS, per CareFirst’s own notification engine). The originating unit currently does not receive any copy.

The need — why the unit should also be notified

Most clinic units we work with operate as the patient’s primary point of care. Practically, this means the unit’s nurse or pharmacist needs to know that a script has been issued so they can:

  • Dispense or hand over medication at the same visit, when the script is issued during a same-day virtual consult.
  • Stock-check / order if the prescribed item isn’t on the shelf.
  • Reconcile the consultation against the unit’s own day-end records.
  • Follow up with the patient if they leave before the script arrives in their inbox.

In our system, each unit has its own contact email (separate from the patient’s and the client-organisation’s). We’d like to provide that unit email on the handoff so CareFirst can include it on the script-issue notification.

What we’re proposing on our side

In our booking system, the operator (system admin) toggles a new per-client setting — “Notify the originating unit on script issue” — on the Client Settings tab, alongside the existing payment-mode and nurse-verification toggles. When that toggle is ON, our handoff request will include the unit’s email as an additional field. When OFF (default), the handoff is unchanged.

To wire that on your side, we need a place in the payload contract to put the unit email. Three approaches that should each be simple to add — we’d like your guidance on which best fits your existing notification engine:

ASimple flat field on the request

You add one top-level optional field, e.g. notifyEmails, which is an array of addresses to CC on every notification CareFirst issues for this booking (including the script email). We’d send [unitEmail].

{
  "uniqueReference": "<booking id>",
  "user": { /* unchanged */ },
  "notifyEmails": ["sandton@clientname.com"]
}

Simplest. Patient-side experience is unchanged.

BStructured per-recipient block

If your engine already supports per-event subscriptions, a structured field gives you finer control (only CC the script, not every email).

{
  "uniqueReference": "...",
  "user": { /* ... */ },
  "additionalRecipients": [
    { "email": "sandton@clientname.com", "events": ["prescription_issued"] }
  ]
}

Slightly more API surface for you, but cleaner semantics.

CWebhook-based (we receive, we forward)

If extending the auto-register payload isn’t feasible, an alternative: CareFirst fires a webhook to a URL we register, including the booking’s uniqueReference and the script PDF/URL. We then forward to the unit email ourselves.

More moving parts on both sides, but useful longer-term — opens the door to other unit-level workflows (e.g. day-end reporting).

Our preference Option A — smallest change, fits the existing single-event model, and we’d implement on our side in roughly half a day once the field name and format are confirmed. We’re happy to go with B or C if it fits your roadmap better.

Questions for CareFirst

Anything you can answer here unblocks us on our side.

  1. Does the auto-register endpoint already support an additional recipient / CC email today (perhaps undocumented)?
    If yes, we can skip the contract change and just start sending the field.
  2. If not, can you add a field on the request — and which of A, B, or C above best fits your notification engine?
    We’ll match whatever shape you prefer.
  3. Which notifications would the additional recipient receive? Just the script email, or also reminders / consultation-summary / other touchpoints?
    Helps us communicate accurately to the unit and to the patient (a CC on every email may feel intrusive; CC only on the script is usually what units want).
  4. Is there a privacy / POPIA consideration on your side we should co-document?
    In our terms, we’ll need to disclose to patients that their script will also be sent to the originating unit. Happy to align the wording with whatever you currently tell patients.
  5. Any rate limits, format constraints (e.g. corporate domains only), or delivery-failure semantics we should handle?
    For example, if the unit email bounces, do you want us to know?
  6. Rough timeline if a contract change is needed?
    Helps us plan the rollout and what to tell clinic operators.
Next step on our side Once we have your answer to Q1 / Q2 we’ll finalise the toggle wiring (it’s already a half-day’s work on our end) and propose a joint test plan in your staging environment before flipping anything on for live clients.
