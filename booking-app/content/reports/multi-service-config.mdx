<Section id="background" num="01 — Background" title="Background">

In our scheduling RFC (<a href="/reports/scheduling-integration">Scheduling Integration — Evaluation Request</a>) we asked whether CareFirst exposes a slot-availability API so we could show real bookable slots to the operator before payment. The response surfaced a related but distinct configuration concern: enabling Scheduled Video on a CareFirst client requires switching that client from **Single-Service** to **Multi-Service** + a corresponding plan adjustment on your side.

For Multi-Service clients, the SSO handoff lands the patient on **CareFirst Patient's Home page** where they pick Instant Video or Scheduled Video themselves — different from Single-Service clients where the handoff goes straight into the consult.

This document captures the design questions that emerged, before we start building anything on our side.

</Section>

<Section id="current" num="02 — Current" title="The current Single-Service assumption">

Today every CareFirst client we integrate against is configured as **Single-Service (Instant Video)**. Our entire flow assumes this:

- Step 4 of patient capture asks for **Booking Type** (Instant or Scheduled)
- For Scheduled bookings we send `bookingType: "scheduled"` + `scheduledAt` in the auto-register payload
- The patient lands directly in the consult on CareFirst Patient (no intermediate page)
- Our booking marks <Pill variant="ok">Successful</Pill> as soon as the handoff returns 2xx

The Scheduled branch is built but **untested end-to-end** — we don't know what your side does with `scheduledAt` today, and the Single-Service assumption may mean it's ignored.

</Section>

<Section id="multi-service" num="03 — Multi-Service" title="What Multi-Service changes (as we understand it)">

For a Multi-Service client:

1. The handoff lands the patient on CareFirst Patient's **Home page**, not the consult
2. The patient chooses **Instant Video** or **Scheduled Video** themselves
3. If Scheduled, the patient picks their slot inside your application
4. The flow then proceeds inside CareFirst Patient

This is fundamentally a different user experience from Single-Service. Implications for us below.

</Section>

<Section id="implications" num="04 — Implications" title="Implications for our flow">

The Multi-Service flow shifts the booking-type decision and slot picking **into CareFirst Patient** rather than our system. Four immediate implications:

<Grid2>
<Card variant="warn" title="Our Step 4 may be redundant for Multi-Service clients">
If the patient picks Instant vs Scheduled on your Home page, our Step 4 ("Booking Type") is asking them the same question twice. We'd want to either skip Step 4 entirely for Multi-Service clients, or keep it as "operator intent" metadata that doesn't bind the patient's actual choice.
</Card>

<Card variant="warn" title="`scheduledAt` may have no effect">
If the patient picks their slot inside your app, the <code>scheduledAt</code> we send in the payload is presumably ignored. We'd want to confirm this and either stop sending it, or have you tell us how to deep-link to a pre-selected slot.
</Card>

<Card variant="warn" title="The patient now sees an extra page">
Single-Service patients see one less page (the Home choice). For Scheduled bookings this is unavoidable today — we have no way to deep-link past it.
</Card>

<Card variant="brand" title="Per-client flag on our side">
Whether a CareFirst client is Single-Service or Multi-Service is something <i>your</i> side decides. We'd want a flag in our <code>clients</code> table to mirror it, so our flow can adjust accordingly per client.
</Card>
</Grid2>

</Section>

<Section id="options" num="05 — Options" title="Three options for the integration shape">

Roughly in order of effort + alignment:

### Option A — Skip Step 4 for Multi-Service clients

We add a flag `is_multi_service` to our `clients` table. When ON:

- Step 4 of patient capture is skipped entirely
- We omit `bookingType` and `scheduledAt` from the auto-register payload
- The patient lands on your Home and picks
- We mark <Pill variant="ok">Successful</Pill> at handoff as today

**Pros:** simplest change on our side; aligns with how Multi-Service is described.<br/>
**Cons:** Operator can't tell the patient "your scheduled slot is locked in" because it isn't — patient still has to pick on your side.

### Option B — Pre-select slot via a slot-list + deep-link API

Requires CareFirst to expose:

1. A slot-list endpoint (covered in the scheduling RFC)
2. A way to deep-link auto-register to **Scheduled Video with a pre-selected slot**, bypassing Home

**Pros:** patient sees one fewer page; operator can confirm slot in person.<br/>
**Cons:** requires more from your side; only viable for Scheduled bookings (Instant doesn't need it).

### Option C — Hybrid

For Multi-Service clients, default to Option A (skip Step 4). Where the operator explicitly knows the patient wants scheduled, they can flip a UI toggle to opt into Option B's deep-link path (assuming you support it).

**Pros:** maximum flexibility, no breaking change.<br/>
**Cons:** more UI complexity on our side.

</Section>

<Section id="questions" num="06 — Questions" title="Open questions for CareFirst">

1. **Multi-Service config & plan adjustment** — what's the lead time / process to flip an existing client? Is it a config change in your admin UI or a code change?
2. **Slot-availability API** — see <a href="/reports/scheduling-integration">scheduling RFC</a>. Now or roadmap?
3. **Deep-link to Scheduled Video with pre-selected slot** — feasible, or does the Home-page selection always need to happen?
4. **Payload fields for Multi-Service** — for Multi-Service clients, should we omit `bookingType` and `scheduledAt`, keep them as metadata, or send something different entirely?
5. **Distinct redirect URLs for Multi-Service Home vs direct consult** — useful for our `returnUrl` handling? (See <a href="/reports/return-url-contract">returnUrl contract</a>.)
6. **Multi-Service identification at our layer** — should we maintain `is_multi_service` as a per-client flag, or can we discover it via a CareFirst API call?

</Section>
