<Section id="framing" num="01 — Framing" title="Framing">

POPIA — the Protection of Personal Information Act — is South Africa's data-protection law. It applies to every booking we process because every patient is a South African data subject (or a foreign visitor whose data is processed in SA). It applies to **both us and CareFirst Patient** for the data we share with you.

This document is partly a reference (what we hold, for how long) and partly an RFC (what we need to align with CareFirst on so we don't end up with conflicting interpretations).

</Section>

<Section id="what-we-hold" num="02 — What we hold" title="What we hold per booking">

Categorised by POPIA's sensitivity ladder:

| Category | Fields | Retention sensitivity |
|---|---|---|
| **Identifying** | Name, ID number, passport, DOB | Personal information |
| **Contact** | Email, phone, address | Personal information |
| **Health** | Vitals (BP, glucose, temp, O₂, heart rate) | **Special personal information** — higher protection |
| **Financial** | Payment amount, payment_type, PayFast reference | Personal information |
| **Behavioural** | Booking timestamps, retry counts, audit trail | Personal information |
| **Consent** | T&Cs acceptance timestamp | Required record |

We do **not** hold:

- Clinical records from the actual consultation (those live with CareFirst)
- Prescription details
- Patient passwords (we have no patient login)
- Card numbers / PAN (PayFast handles that)

</Section>

<Section id="how-long" num="03 — How long" title="How long we hold it">

Currently we hold **everything indefinitely**. We have a retention sweep mechanism designed but not yet enforcing — that's the gap to close.

### Designed retention policy (proposed, not yet enforced)

| Data category | Retention | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Identifying + Contact fields on a Successful booking | 7 years after consultation | Healthcare records-retention norm |
| Health (vitals) | 7 years after consultation | Same |
| Financial | 7 years after payment | SARS records-retention |
| Audit log | Indefinite | Compliance evidence |
| Bookings in <Pill variant="mute">Abandoned</Pill> / <Pill variant="mute">Discarded</Pill> | 90 days | No legitimate basis to keep longer |

### Retention sweep mechanism

A scheduled job that **nulls PII** on bookings past their retention window. Audit log row IDs survive (compliance), but the joined data (`actor_name`, `entity_name`, `changes.old/new`) is redacted to `[redacted-retention]`.

</Section>

<Section id="data-flow" num="04 — Data flow" title="Data flow to CareFirst — what crosses the boundary">

The SSO auto-register payload (see [SSO contract](/reports/sso-auto-register)) transfers personal information to CareFirst. Specifically:

**Always sent:**
- Identity (name, ID, DOB, gender, title)
- Contact (email, phone, address)
- Booking reference

**Never sent today:**
- Vitals (health information — see [Vitals Capture](/reports/vitals-capture))
- Payment details
- Audit trail
- IP addresses or device fingerprints

The boundary at the auto-register POST is the **point of joint controllership** under POPIA. From that POST onward, both parties hold the data; both have obligations to the data subject.

</Section>

<Section id="shared" num="05 — Joint controller" title="Joint-controller implications">

When two parties hold the same personal data, POPIA expects:

- A **written agreement** describing each party's responsibilities
- A **single point of contact** for data-subject queries
- **Aligned retention policies** — if you keep something for 10 years and we delete at 7, the data subject doesn't have a clean answer to "do you still have my information?"
- **Coordinated breach notification** — if your side is breached, our patients are affected; we need to know in time to inform them

We don't have a written joint-controller agreement today. **That's an actionable gap on both sides.**

</Section>

<Section id="erasure" num="06 — Erasure" title="Right to erasure">

A data subject can request their data be erased from our system. Our flow:

1. Subject submits a request (via email today; structured form planned)
2. system_admin verifies identity
3. Bookings are looked up by `id_number` / `email_address`
4. We run an admin-gated erasure endpoint that:
   - Nulls PII columns on the matching bookings
   - Audit-logs the erasure event
   - Returns the list of booking UUIDs affected

**The problem:** if those bookings have already been handed off to CareFirst, you also hold the patient's data. The subject's right to erasure extends to you too — but our erasure endpoint doesn't notify your side today.

Suggested integration: a `DELETE /api/external/patient/<referenceId>` call (or similar) from us to you, fired during our erasure flow. We'd send the `external_reference_id` we have stored; you'd act on your side.

</Section>

<Section id="subject-access" num="07 — Subject access" title="Subject access requests">

Subjects can also request a copy of all data we hold on them. Our flow:

1. Subject submits a request
2. system_admin verifies identity
3. We dump all bookings + audit-log rows matching their `id_number` / `email_address`
4. Output is a structured JSON file we email to the subject

If the subject also wants their CareFirst Patient consultation data, that's a separate request to your side — but we should be able to **direct them appropriately**. Today we don't surface the external reference IDs we hold to the subject, but we could.

</Section>

<Section id="questions" num="08 — Questions" title="Open questions for CareFirst">

1. **Joint-controller agreement.** Do you have a template, or should we draft? Either way, this should land before more clients onboard.
2. **Retention alignment.** What's your retention policy for data we hand off? Aligning at 7 years would simplify subject conversations.
3. **Erasure propagation API.** Would you accept a `DELETE` call (or webhook) from us when a subject requests erasure on our side? What endpoint shape works for you?
4. **Reverse erasure.** If a subject requests erasure from your side first, can you notify us so we can mirror? Suggested: same shape as our outbound — `POST /api/webhooks/carefirst/erasure-request`.
5. **Breach notification channel.** Is there a designated email / Slack / contact for breach coordination? Should be documented before we need it.
6. **Cross-system subject access.** Should a subject access request to one party include the other party's data? Or do we direct them to each system independently?
7. **Special-category protection.** Vitals (if we start sending them) are health data. Acceptable channel for transfer? Any additional encryption-at-rest commitments needed on your side?

</Section>
